# Captain.ai — Figma Make Prompt v2
## Mood board–matched: warm, magical, surrealist, comforting

---

## PROMPT

Design a full landing page for Captain.ai — an AI workflow orchestration tool for small teams. The design style should feel warm, magical, and surrealist — like a sunrise through frosted glass. NOT a typical dark SaaS page. Think dreamy and human, not cold and techy.

**Visual style (CRITICAL — follow closely):**
- Background: soft warm gradient base — light lavender (#E8E0F0) blending into warm cream (#FFF8F0) and pale peach (#FFE8DD). The page should feel like it's glowing softly.
- Use large, organic gradient blob shapes as decorative background elements — soft abstract forms in lavender, peach, coral, and warm orange, with blurred edges. Similar to the aurora/silhouette art style. These should be subtle and atmospheric, not sharp.
- Cards and containers: frosted glass / glassmorphism effect — semi-transparent white backgrounds (rgba 255,255,255,0.4) with a subtle backdrop blur, soft rounded corners (16-24px), and very light borders.
- Buttons: gradient fills (lavender-to-peach or indigo-to-violet), pill-shaped with generous padding. Primary CTA should glow slightly.
- Color palette: deep indigo (#3B3680) for headlines and primary text, soft lavender (#B8A9D4) for accents, warm peach (#F4A77A) for highlights, coral rose (#E8637A) for emphasis, muted sage teal (#7BBAB5) for secondary elements. Body text in dark warm gray (#2D2A3E).
- Typography: use a clean sans-serif (Inter or Plus Jakarta Sans) for body text. For the main headline, use a slightly stylized or italic serif feel if possible — elegant, not corporate. Keep it readable but with personality.
- Generous whitespace. Sections should feel spacious and airy, like floating.
- Rounded corners on everything — buttons, cards, images, containers. Nothing sharp.
- Subtle soft shadows (large blur, low opacity, warm tint) rather than hard drop shadows.
- NO dark backgrounds. NO sharp geometric patterns. NO corporate blue. The whole page should feel like a warm, calming, magical workspace.

**Page structure — design these sections in order:**

**1. Navigation bar**
Floating nav with glassmorphism background. Logo text "Captain.ai" on the left in indigo, with italic styling or a subtle mark. Nav links centered: How It Works, Integrations, Use Cases, Pricing. Right side: "Get Early Access" pill button with a lavender-to-peach gradient fill. The nav should feel like it's floating over the background.

**2. Hero section**
Large headline in deep indigo, bold with slight personality: "Tell your team what needs to get done. Captain handles everything else."
Subheadline in warm gray below: "Captain is your AI control layer. Type a task — Captain breaks it into steps, assigns each to the right person or AI agent, and runs the whole workflow end-to-end."
Primary CTA: "Get Early Access" pill button with gradient (indigo to violet), slightly glowing. Secondary: "Watch it work ↓" as a text link in soft indigo.
Small proof line below: "Built for teams of 3–15 using AI agents every day."
Behind the text, place a large, soft abstract gradient blob — a warm organic shape in peach, lavender, and orange, blurred, like the aurora silhouette images from the mood board. The hero should feel dreamy and expansive. Lots of vertical space.

**3. Problem section — "The Bottleneck"**
Small uppercase label in coral rose: "THE BOTTLENECK ISN'T EXECUTION"
Headline in indigo: "Your team has 10 AI tools. Nobody's flying the plane."
Two short paragraphs explaining that individual AI tools are fast but team coordination is broken. Who plans the workflow? Who decides which agent handles which step? Right now that's the founder, in Slack, at midnight.
Two stat callouts in frosted glass cards, side by side:
- "58%" in large text with peach/coral gradient coloring, label below: "of your day lost to coordination"
- "67%" in large text with lavender/indigo gradient coloring, label below: "of strategies that fail from execution drift"
The stat numbers should be big and slightly gradient-colored to match the warm palette.

**4. How It Works section**
Small uppercase label in sage teal: "HOW CAPTAIN WORKS"
Headline: "One input. A complete plan. Automatic execution."
Three steps displayed as horizontal frosted glass cards with rounded corners:
- Step 1: "Describe what you need" — icon of a text input or chat bubble. "Type a task in plain language. No tickets, no subtasks."
- Step 2: "Captain builds the plan" — icon of a branching workflow. "Captain generates a step-by-step workflow, assigning each step to the right AI agent or human."
- Step 3: "Humans and AI execute together" — icon of people and robots collaborating. "AI steps run automatically. Human steps land in their inbox with full context."
Each card should have a soft number (01, 02, 03) in a large, light watermark style, a small icon, bold title, and description. Cards should feel like they're floating with the glassmorphism effect.

**5. Workflow in Action section**
Small uppercase label: "SEE IT WORK"
Headline: "'Make a pitch deck' → 7 steps → done."
Show a vertical timeline of workflow steps, each as a small glassmorphism card:
Step 1: "AI · OpenSpec" — Fetch documentation and technical specs.
Step 2: "AI · OpenDevin" — Analyze competitive landscape.
Step 3: "AI · OpenSpec" — Generate slide-by-slide outline.
Step 4: "HUMAN · Sunny" — Review outline for tone, accuracy, brand alignment.
Step 5: "AI · Gamma" — Generate visual pitch deck with layouts.
Step 6: "AI · Decktopus" — Create data-driven market and financial slides.
Step 7: "HUMAN · Sunny" — Final review before shipping.
Each step card should have a colored badge: soft green/teal for AI steps, warm peach/coral for HUMAN steps. A subtle connecting line runs between steps. The whole thing should feel like a beautiful, flowing sequence.
Below the timeline: "Captain planned 7 steps across 4 AI agents and 2 humans — from one sentence."

**6. Capabilities section**
Small uppercase label: "WHAT CAPTAIN DOES"
Headline: "Plan. Route. Execute. Oversee."
Four feature cards in a 2x2 grid, each with glassmorphism styling:
- "Plan" — Turn any task into a multi-step workflow with the right agents and humans for each step. Use a soft indigo icon.
- "Route & Execute" — Send each step to the right executor: OpenAI for research, Gamma for decks, a team member for decisions. Use a warm peach icon.
- "Wait for Humans" — When a step needs human judgment, Captain pauses and surfaces the right context. No AI running ahead unsupervised. Use a coral rose icon.
- "Oversee" — Real-time dashboard showing what's running, blocked, and done across every person and agent. Use a sage teal icon.
Each card: rounded icon with matching accent color, bold title, 2 lines of description on a frosted glass background.

**7. Integrations section**
Small uppercase label: "CONNECTS TO EVERYTHING"
Headline: "Your AI agents. Your tools. One control layer."
Two rows of integration logos on frosted glass pill badges:
Row 1 — AI Models: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Perplexity, DeepSeek
Row 2 — Tools: Notion, Slack, GitHub, Gmail, Google Drive, Linear, Gamma, Supabase, Vercel, Calendly
Below: "Captain doesn't replace your tools. It makes them work together." in warm gray italic.
Keep this section clean and compact with soft, rounded logo badges.

**8. Built For section**
Small uppercase label: "BUILT FOR"
Headline: "Small teams that punch above their weight."
Three persona cards (horizontal, glassmorphism):
- "The Founding Team" — 5 people doing the work of 20. Captain becomes the coordination layer you can't hire yet.
- "The AI-First Dev Team" — Shipping is fast. Knowing what to ship next is the hard part. Captain handles that.
- "The Lean Growth Team" — AI outputs that need human review before going live. Captain automates the workflow and keeps humans in the loop.
Each card has a soft gradient accent at the top edge (different color per card: lavender, peach, teal).

**9. Vision section**
Slightly different background — a deeper lavender or soft twilight gradient to signal "looking forward."
Small uppercase label: "WHERE THIS IS GOING"
Headline: "The operating system for human + AI teams."
Short paragraph about the three phases: today Captain is an AI control layer. Tomorrow it becomes the team brain. The future is a full work OS for human+AI teams.
Three phase labels displayed as pill badges in a horizontal row:
"Now: Control Layer" → "Next: Team Brain" → "Future: Work OS"
with subtle connecting arrows and gradient coloring from peach (now) to lavender (next) to indigo (future).

**10. Final CTA section**
Large centered headline: "Every task starts here."
Subheadline: "Tell Captain what needs to get done. Let your humans and AI agents do the rest."
Large pill CTA button: "Get Early Access" with glowing gradient effect.
Small text: "Free for early teams. We'll onboard you personally."
Background: a large, soft gradient blob behind the CTA, creating a warm focal point — like the sunset gradients in the mood board.

**11. Footer**
Clean, minimal. Logo "Captain.ai" on left. Links: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Blog, Docs, Contact. Right: Privacy, Terms.
Bottom: "© 2026 Captain.ai — The control layer for human + AI teams."
Soft lavender background, slightly separated from the CTA section.

**Overall feel:**
This page should feel like stepping into a warm, calming, magical space — not a corporate SaaS dashboard. Think of it as the opposite of the typical dark, techy AI landing page. It should feel human, approachable, and beautiful. Like the product cares about people as much as it cares about productivity. The glassmorphism and soft gradients should make everything feel like it's floating in a gentle, luminous environment. The warm color palette (lavenders, peaches, corals) should make the visitor feel comfortable and curious, not intimidated. This is a tool that makes humans and AI work together — and the design should feel like that collaboration: warm intelligence.

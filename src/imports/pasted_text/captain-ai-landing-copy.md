## Section 3: HOW IT WORKS

### Section label
HOW CAPTAIN WORKS

### Headline
One input. A complete plan. Automatic execution.

### Three-step flow

**Step 1: Describe what you need**
Type a task in plain language: "Make a pitch deck for our Series A."
"Conduct market research on competitor X." "Build and deploy a landing page."
No tickets. No subtasks. Just say what done looks like.

**Step 2: Captain builds the plan**
Captain analyzes your task, pulls context from your connected tools, and generates
a step-by-step workflow. Each step is assigned to the best executor — an AI agent
like OpenAI, Manus, or Gamma, or a human team member like you.

**Step 3: Humans and AI execute together**
AI agents run their steps automatically. When Captain needs a human decision —
a review, an approval, a creative judgment call — it routes the task to their inbox
with full context. No one is blocked. Nothing is missed.

### Four capability cards (2x2 grid)

**Plan**
Turn any task into a clear, multi-step workflow. Captain analyzes your request,
pulls context from connected tools, and builds an execution plan —
complete with the right agents and humans for each step.

**Route & Execute**
Captain sends each step to the right executor — whether that's OpenAI for research,

# MESSAGING STRATEGY NOTES (v2)

## Core repositioning
The research docs positioned Captain as a "commitment tracker" and Slack coordination tool.
The actual product is much bigger: it's a **workflow orchestration engine** that plans,
routes, and oversees multi-step workflows across humans and AI agents.

The landing page should sell THIS — the product people will actually use.

## Key message hierarchy (revised)
1. **One input, full execution** — the magic is typing a task and getting a complete plan (demo hook)
2. **Humans + AI as one team** — steps assigned to the right executor, human or AI (differentiation)
3. **Human-in-the-loop by design** — Captain pauses for human judgment, doesn't automate blindly (trust)
4. **Connects to everything** — works with the tools and AI models you already use (low friction)
5. **Built for small teams** — 3–15 people, not enterprise (targeting)

## The "wow" moment to communicate
User types: "Make a pitch deck on Open Claw product"
Captain generates 7 steps across 4 AI agents and 2 humans.
Workflow runs. AI agents execute. Humans review at the right moments.
Pitch deck ships.

THAT is the demo. That is what the landing page needs to make people feel.

## Competitive positioning (revised)
- vs. Asana/Monday: "Those manage tasks. Captain executes them."
- vs. CrewAI/LangGraph: "Those are frameworks for developers. Captain is a product for teams."
- vs. Slack AI: "Slack AI searches your messages. Captain turns them into executed workflows."
- vs. Zapier/Make: "Those connect triggers to actions. Captain plans and orchestrates entire projects."

## Words to USE
- Control layer, orchestrate, workflow, plan, route, execute, oversee
- "Humans and AI, working as one team"
- "One input. Done."
- "Human-in-the-loop" / "pauses for human judgment"
- Team Brain, AI control layer

## Words to AVOID
- "Project management" (old category)
- "Automation" alone (implies no human involvement)
- "AI agent framework" (too technical, developer-focused)
- "Chatbot" or "assistant" (undersells what Captain does)
- "Productivity" (meaningless at this point)

## Tone
Confident, precise, slightly futuristic but grounded. Like a mission briefing,
not a sales pitch. The product name is "Captain" — lean into the metaphor
of someone competent flying the plane while the crew does their jobs.

# Captain.ai — Figma Make Prompt

Copy and paste the prompt below into Figma Make. It's written to give the AI enough structure and visual direction to generate a strong first draft you can then refine.

---

## PROMPT

Design a full landing page for Captain.ai — an AI Chief of Staff product for small teams (3–15 people). The page should feel like Linear.app or Raycast.com: dark, minimal, confident, developer-oriented, with generous whitespace and sharp typography.

**Visual style:**
- Dark background (#0A0A0F or similar near-black)
- White primary text, light gray (#A0A0A0) for secondary text
- One accent color: electric indigo/violet (#6366F1) for buttons and highlights
- Clean sans-serif font (Inter or similar)
- Generous spacing between sections. Let the page breathe.
- No gradients, no glassmorphism, no decorative illustrations. Clarity over decoration.
- Subtle grid lines or dot patterns as background texture are fine.

**Page structure — design these sections in order:**

**1. Navigation bar**
Logo text "Captain.ai" on the left. Links: How It Works, For Teams, Pricing, Blog. Right side: "Get Early Access" button in accent color.

**2. Hero section**
Large headline in bold white: "Your AI team ships fast. Your coordination doesn't."
Subheadline in gray below: "Captain.ai lives in your Slack. It catches every commitment, tracks every thread, and makes sure nothing falls through the cracks — so your team stays aligned without a single status update."
Primary CTA button: "Join the Waitlist" in accent color. Secondary text link: "See how it works ↓"
Keep this section very spacious — at least 80vh height. Center-aligned text.

**3. Problem section — "The Coordination Tax"**
Small uppercase label: "THE COORDINATION TAX"
Headline: "AI made you faster. It didn't make your team more aligned."
Two short paragraphs of body text explaining that individual AI tools accelerate execution but team coordination is getting worse. Commitments get lost in Slack, decisions are forgotten, AI agents finish outdated tasks.
Three large stat callouts displayed in a row:
- "58%" with label "of your day spent on work about work"
- "67%" with label "of strategies fail due to execution drift"
- "1.8 hrs/day" with label "spent searching for info that already exists"
The numbers should be very large and in accent color. Labels in small gray text below.

**4. How It Works section**
Small uppercase label: "HOW CAPTAIN WORKS"
Headline: "Zero setup. Zero status updates. Just clarity."
Three steps displayed horizontally as cards or a timeline:
- Step 1: "Captain listens" — connects to Slack and meeting transcripts, no configuration needed.
- Step 2: "Captain catches commitments" — automatically identifies who promised what to whom and when it's due.
- Step 3: "Captain closes the loop" — gentle nudges to the right person, confirms completion, nothing falls through.
Each step should have a number or icon, a bold title, and 1–2 lines of description.

**5. Capabilities section**
Small uppercase label: "WHAT CAPTAIN DOES"
Headline: "Everything a Chief of Staff does. Nothing you have to maintain."
Four feature cards in a 2x2 grid:
- "Commitment Tracker" — auto-extracts promises from conversations and follows up until done.
- "Unified Team Dashboard" — see what every team member and AI agent is working on, one view.
- "Zero-Entry Coordination" — work items create themselves from Slack and meeting notes.
- "Intent Alignment" — tracks whether execution still matches original decisions.
Each card: subtle border or slight background lift, icon placeholder, bold title, 2 lines of description.

**6. Built For section**
Small uppercase label: "BUILT FOR"
Headline: "AI-native teams that are too fast for project management tools."
One line of subtext: "Captain.ai is for teams of 3–15 who use AI agents daily and don't have time for Jira."
Three persona cards side by side:
- "The Founding Team" — you're CEO, PM, and head of sales. Captain keeps everything tracked.
- "The AI-Augmented Dev Team" — engineers use Cursor and Copilot, code ships fast, coordination hasn't kept up.
- "The Lean Startup" — 8 people, can't afford a COO, but need that level of organizational clarity.

**7. Vision section**
Small uppercase label: "WHERE WE'RE GOING"
Headline: "The operating system for human + AI teams."
Short paragraph: today Captain is a digital Chief of Staff. Tomorrow it becomes the shared brain connecting every person and AI agent to the same source of truth.
Consider showing a subtle three-phase evolution: "Chief of Staff → Team Brain → Work OS"
This section can have a slightly lighter background to create contrast.

**8. Final CTA section**
Large headline centered: "Stop coordinating. Start shipping."
Subheadline: "Join the waitlist for early access. We're onboarding design partners now."
Primary CTA button: "Get Early Access"
Small text below: "No credit card required. We'll reach out personally."

**9. Footer**
Logo text "Captain.ai" on left. Links: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Blog, Contact. Right side: Privacy Policy, Terms.
Bottom line: "© 2026 Captain.ai — Coordination for the AI age."

**Overall feel:**
Think Linear meets Vercel. Dark, fast, smart. The page should feel like it was designed by the same kind of team that would use this product — small, sharp, taste-driven. No clutter, no corporate feel, no stock photography. Every element earns its place.
